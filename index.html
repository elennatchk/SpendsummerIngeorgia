About Georgia

What is Georgia ?

Georgia is one of the oldest wine-producing countries in the world; local winemaking traditions are entwined with Georgia's national identity, and have been classified by UNESCO as part of the world's Intangible Cultural Heritage.

What Georgia Offers for Tourists ?

It's all about nature here. Despite its small size, Georgia has all kinds of climate zones including deserts, ski resorts, Black Sea coasts and alpine zones. The country is a paradise for hiking and camping lovers or for those who simply enjoy stunning views of the Caucasus Mountain Range.

Mtskheta

Mtskheta is one of the oldest cities of Georgia and its former capital. Currently a small provincial capital, for nearly a millennium until the 5th century AD, Mtskheta was a large fortified city, a significant economical centre of the Kingdom of Iberia

Svaneti

Svaneti is a historic province of Georgia, in the northwestern part of the country. It is inhabited by the Svans, an ethnic subgroup of Georgians. The landscape of Svaneti is dominated by mountains that are separated by deep gorges.

Georgian Dishes

Khinkali

Khinkali is a Georgian dumpling, which originated in the Georgian mountain regions of Pshavi, Tusheti and Khevsureti. The original recipe, the so-called khevsuruli, consisted of only minced meat , onions, chili pepper, salt, and cumin.

Adjarian Khatchapuri

Khachapuri is a traditional Georgian dish of cheese-filled bread. The bread is leavened and allowed to rise and is shaped in various ways, usually with cheese in the middle and a crust which is ripped off and used to dip in the cheese.

Chakapuli

Chakapuli is a Georgian dish. It is considered to be one of the most popular dishes in Georgia. It is made from lamb chops , onions, tarragon leaves,tkemali, white wine, garlic and salt. Chakapuli can also be made with mushrooms instead of lamb.
